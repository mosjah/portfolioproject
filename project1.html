Brief

This is Wolverine Wellness, an app designed to provide Mental Health Solutions at Your Fingertips for the University of Michigan (UM) community. As a class project, I took on the challenge of addressing the mental health struggles that I and my peers face, such as academic pressure, social isolation, and stigma surrounding mental health. Inspired by survey data, personal experiences, and conversations with friends, I wanted to create a solution that fosters peer connections and offers practical tools for improving mental health. The result: Wolverine Wellness—a resource center that combines accessibility, personalized support, and peer-to-peer community building.

Features: A Better Way to Support Mental Health

Wolverine Wellness is designed with three key features to address students' mental health challenges:

  1. Connect with Peers: Build connections with others who understand their experiences, reducing isolation and creating a support system.
  2. Monitor Your Mood: Track emotional patterns to better understand mental health journeys and identify trends over time.
  3. Access Personalized Resources: Receive tailored strategies and tools to manage mental health challenges, from stress management to peer support.

Research

Suggestions: Solidifying the Direction

Based on feedback and analysis, I refined the direction with these key improvements:

  • Flow Improvements:
    • Goals became the central navigation hub, making the user experience more intuitive.
    • A calendar view was added to visualize mood trends and milestones.
  • Usability Enhancements:
    • Improved onboarding to better guide first-time users.
    • Added labels and visual cues to simplify navigation and interactions.
  • Feature Expansion:
    • Enabled multiple peer connections to ensure users always had support.
    • Integrated anonymous discussion spaces to reduce stigma and foster openness.

Final Product

The final design embodies these key elements:

  1. Design Choices: The blue and maize color scheme was chosen for its association with UM.
  2. Core Features: Peer-to-peer connections, mood tracking, and a safe, anonymous community feed.
  3. User-Centric Approach: Every feature was tested and refined to address the specific frustrations and goals of UM students, ensuring accessibility, inclusivity, and reliability.

Reflections

Through the development of Wolverine Wellness, I learned the value of user-centered design and the importance of bridging gaps in traditional mental health resources with innovative, community-driven solutions. This project demonstrated how thoughtful design can empower students to take charge of their mental health while fostering a sense of connection and support.
